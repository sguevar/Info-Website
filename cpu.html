2. Central Processing Unit (CPU) / Processor

The Processor (CPU) of a computer is responsible for listening and sending instructions from other components and to others. The CPU is like the brain of a computer, without one a computer will not function. Similar to a motherboard, you need to have a CPU in order to have a working computer but the CPU is seen as more important. One major component people look at to determine the speed and power of a computer is the processor that it has. Being able to send instructions to the rest of your components at a faster pace will determine how good a computer can run. The only two manufacturers of CPUs for computers are Intel and AMD.
